About CODE128 barcodes

CODE128 is a high-density 1D (linear) barcode that can encode all 128 ASCII characters — uppercase and lowercase letters, digits, and symbols. It is widely used in shipping, logistics, packaging, and supply-chain labeling, and it is the basis of the GS1-128 standard. This tool uses CODE128 in auto mode, which automatically selects the most compact subset (A, B, or C) for your data.

About QR codes

A QR code (Quick Response code) is a 2D matrix barcode that holds much more data than a linear barcode and can be scanned from any direction by a smartphone camera. QR codes support four error-correction levels (L, M, Q, H) so they still scan even when partly damaged, and they can store URLs, plain text, contact cards, Wi-Fi credentials, and more — including Korean, Japanese, and Chinese characters.

Barcode vs. QR code

Use a CODE128 barcode for short alphanumeric IDs in retail and logistics where 1D scanners are standard. Use a QR code when you need to store URLs, longer text, non-English characters, or want easy scanning with any phone. With this generator you can switch between the two instantly and compare.

Frequently asked questions

How do I generate multiple barcodes at once?

Paste your values one per line; each line becomes its own code in the grid. Empty lines are ignored and surrounding spaces are trimmed.

Is it really free?

Yes — completely free, no account or installation required.

Why does a barcode show an error?

CODE128 supports ASCII only. If a value contains characters it cannot encode (for example Korean), only that cell shows an error — switch to QR mode for such values.

Can I use it offline / is my data safe?

Codes are generated entirely in your browser. The values you type are never uploaded to a server.
