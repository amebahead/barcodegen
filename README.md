## Barcodes & QR codes 101

**CODE128** is a high-density **1D (linear) barcode** that encodes all 128 ASCII characters (letters, digits, symbols). It's a backbone of shipping, logistics, packaging, and supply-chain labeling, and underpins the **GS1-128** standard. This tool uses CODE128 in **auto** mode, picking the most compact subset (A/B/C) automatically.

**QR codes** (Quick Response codes) are **2D matrix barcodes** that store far more data than linear barcodes and scan from any angle with a smartphone camera. They offer four **error-correction** levels (L, M, Q, H) so they still scan when partly damaged, and they can hold URLs, text, contact cards, Wi-Fi credentials, and multi-byte characters (Korean, Japanese, Chinese).

**Barcode vs QR:** use a **CODE128 barcode** for short alphanumeric IDs with 1D scanners (retail/logistics); use a **QR code** for URLs, longer or non-English text, and easy phone scanning.

## FAQ

**How do I generate multiple barcodes at once?**
Paste your values one per line; each line becomes its own code in the grid. Empty lines are ignored and spaces are trimmed.

**Is it free?**
Yes — completely free, no account or installation.

**Which barcode format?**

Yes — PNG per code, all as a ZIP, or Print → "Save as PDF".
